# Prompt Engineering Verbs

When describing the workflow of using a prompt to transform raw inputs into a final AI-generated output (e.g., turning raw meeting notes into a presentation deck via an LLM), use these precise English verbs depending on the nuance required:

## 1. Emphasizing Logical Integration (Professional & Grounded)

- **Synthesize**: The most common and accurate term in AI workflows. It implies taking fragmented, multi-source raw materials, blending them via internal logic, and producing a cohesive new artifact.
  - _Example_: "We use a specific prompt to **synthesize** raw materials into a cohesive presentation."
- **Compile**: A structured, engineering-focused term. It implies gathering and formatting data to feed into the AI.
- **Assimilate**: Emphasizes the AI "digesting" and absorbing the formatting constraints of the prompt.

## 2. Emphasizing Control & Architecture (High-Level / Advanced)

- **Orchestrate**: A highly professional business/tech term. It treats the prompt as the "conductor" directing the AI to turn source texts into a structured output.
  - _Example_: "We **orchestrate** the AI using dynamic prompts to turn source texts into a structured pitch deck."
- **Formulate**: Emphasizes the creation of the prompt itself as a logical "recipe" or formula.

## 3. Emphasizing Reduction & Clarity (For Long-to-Short Workflows)

- **Distill**: The best choice when the raw input is massive and the output must be concise (e.g., 50 pages of docs into 10 slides).
  - _Example_: "The prompt directs the AI to **distill** 50 pages of documentation into a 10-slide presentation."
- **Digest**: Similar to distill, emphasizing simplification of complex information.

## 4. Emphasizing Creativity & Seamless Blending

- **Weave**: Highly visual; treats the prompt as the loom and the data as threads.
- **Catalyze**: Treats the prompt as the chemical catalyst that sparks the AI's generation process.
